The Script · Run It Every Time

Four questions that do the selling for you.

You never pitch. You diagnose. By the time the iPad turns around, the customer has already sold themselves.

1
Q1 · The Hook

“There is another discount that you qualify for, and it's our mobile discount. Who do you have your cell phones with?”

The move · Diagnose, don't pitch

Opening with a question makes you the doctor, not the salesperson — a good doctor asks what's wrong before handing over a pill. And “a discount you qualify for” feels earned, not sold. You're not asking them to buy anything yet, so their guard drops and they start talking.

🧠SPIN Selling (Rackham): success in larger sales depends, more than anything else, on how the investigating stage is handled.
2
Q2 · Qualify The Lines

“Great! How many lines do you have?”

The move · Asking builds trust

Say “Great!” like their answer is exactly what you were hoping to hear — because it is. Every carrier name is the right answer. Simple questions make people feel heard; treat every reply as “perfect” so they stay relaxed, and now you know exactly how many lines to quote.

🧠Gong.io, 100,000+ sales calls: the best reps are listening — not talking — 57% of the time.
3
Q3 · Find The Pain

“How much does your cell phone bill cost you each month?”

The move · Loss aversion

When the customer says their bill out loud, the overpaying suddenly feels like real money walking out the door every month. People hate losing money about 2× as much as they enjoy saving it — so that number does the work for you. You never knock their carrier; you just help them feel a problem they already had.

🧠Kahneman & Tversky, Prospect Theory: “Losses loom larger than gains.”
4
Q4 · The Mic Drop 🎤The Big One

“Why are you paying so much?”

The move · Self-persuasion

Then pause — and let them answer. People believe their own words far more than yours. When they explain why they're overpaying, they talk themselves into the problem — you didn't push it. And whatever they say is the exact objection you'll have to beat, so you learn it before you ever pitch.

🧠SPIN Selling (Rackham): “If you can get your customers to say, ‘I want it,’ it's not difficult… to reply, ‘We can give it to you.’”
The Science · Framing

The words you choose do the selling.

Same facts, different words — a different decision.

“we use their towers”→“partnered with Verizon”Partnering borrows Verizon's premium reputation. “Towers” makes us sound like the cheap version.
“let me sell you”→“a discount you qualify for”“Discount” and “qualify” make it feel earned, not sold. That builds trust.
knocking their carrier→respecting their choicePeople defend choices they've already made. Never knock the carrier — beat the bill instead.
